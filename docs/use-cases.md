# Who It's For

Siklab Core is for people who need working software but don't have a development team — or don't want to stand one up just to find out if an idea works. You describe what you need; Siklab builds a real, working version you can use, show, and keep.

---

## The solo founder

**You have the idea, not the dev budget.**

Hiring engineers, learning to code, or wrestling a no-code tool into shape are all months and money you don't have yet. You need to get something real in front of people _now_ — to test the idea, show a first user, or start.

With Siklab you describe your product in plain language, refine it against a live preview, and walk away with a working first version. When feedback comes in, you change it the same way you built it — by asking.

> _"A simple booking page where clients pick a time slot and I get notified."_ → a working page you can share the same day.

---

## The agency owner or freelancer

**You build for clients, and speed is the product.**

A client wants to _see_ it, not read a spec. Standing up a project, a repo, and a sprint just to show a first cut is overhead you eat before you've been paid for the real work.

Siklab lets you turn a client conversation into a working app in an afternoon — then iterate on their feedback live, in front of them. Deliver the first version fast, keep the relationship moving, and spend your senior time where it actually matters.

> _"An intake form for my client's studio, branded to their colors, that collects lead details."_ → a polished, working form to hand over.

---

## The product manager

**You need a POC before you spend a dev-week.**

Before engineering commits, you have to prove the concept, pressure-test a flow, or get stakeholders aligned on something they can actually click — not a slide.

Siklab gives you a real, interactive proof of concept in the time it used to take to mock one up. Validate the idea, kill it cheaply if it's wrong, or walk into the roadmap conversation with a working prototype instead of a pitch.

> _"A prototype of the new onboarding flow so the team can click through it before we build it."_ → an interactive POC to align everyone.

---

## The small-business owner or operator

**You need the specific tool your workflow actually wants.**

Off-the-shelf software makes you bend your process to fit it. The tracker, form, or simple dashboard you really need is too small to hire for and too particular to buy.

Siklab builds the exact tool — an inventory list, an expense log, a job tracker, a simple internal dashboard — shaped to how you actually work, not how a generic product assumes you do.

> _"A tracker for my food truck's daily glasses sold, with a running total and a reset each day."_ → a tool built for your day, not someone else's.

---

## What it's great at

Siklab shines at **self-contained web apps and tools** — the first working version, the prototype, the internal tool, the thing you'd otherwise never get built. You describe it, refine it live, and own the result.
